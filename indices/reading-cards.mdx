Every index card on the [Indices](/indices/overview) page packs a lot of signal into a small box. Here's how to decode it.

## Anatomy of a card

- **Ticker + name** — top-left (e.g., `^GSPC` / S&P 500)
- **Current price** — large, top
- **Change %** — color-coded green/red against the active timeframe
- **Sparkline** — the price path over the active timeframe
- **Percentile shading** — background tint based on where today sits in the trailing 1-year distribution
- **Sigma badge** — appears only at extremes (`+2σ`, `+1.5σ`, `−1.5σ`, `−2σ`)

## Timeframes

The "usual timeframes" toggle at the top of the page applies to all cards at once:

| Tab | Window |
|---|---|
| **1D** | Today |
| **1W** | 5 trading days |
| **1M** | 21 trading days |
| **3M** | 63 trading days |
| **6M** | 126 trading days |
| **YTD** | Since Jan 1 |
| **1Y** | 252 trading days |
| **3Y** | 756 trading days |

Switch the timeframe to ask different questions — 1D for "what happened today," 1Y for "where are we in the regime."

## Percentile shading

The card background is tinted based on the **1-year percentile** of today's reading.

- **Deep green** = top decile (95th+ percentile)
- **Light green** = upper quartile
- **Neutral** = middle 50%
- **Light red** = lower quartile
- **Deep red** = bottom decile (5th– percentile)

Useful for spotting outliers in a glance — a deep-red card on a green tape is asking why.

## Sigma badges

Appear only when the reading is statistically extreme on its 1-year distribution:

| Badge | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **+2σ** | 95th+ percentile — multi-month high |
| **+1.5σ** | 84th+ percentile — elevated |
| **−1.5σ** | 16th– percentile — depressed |
| **−2σ** | 5th– percentile — multi-month low |

These are mean-reversion flags, not trade signals. They tell you the reading is unusual, not that it's about to revert.

## Click into a card

Click any index card to open a detail view with:
- Larger chart with full timeframe history
- Range stats (high/low, % from highs)
- Related instruments

## How most people use the cards

- **Eye sweep** — scan all 25 for outlier shading and sigma badges
- **Cross-region** — compare same-timeframe shading across Americas / Europe / Asia
- **Click in** — when a card stands out, click to see the chart and ask why
