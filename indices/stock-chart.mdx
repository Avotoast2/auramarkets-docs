Anywhere a single stock shows up on the [Equities](/indices/overview) page — **Top Gainers / Top Losers**, [New Highs / Lows](/indices/highs-lows), [Moving Averages](/indices/moving-averages) — there's a little chart icon next to it. Click it and the **stock pop-out** opens: a chart on the right, and a collapsible company panel on the left. It's a mini terminal for that name without leaving the page.

<Note>
The chart, company panel, and research section are powered by our equity data feeds and cover **US-listed stocks and ETFs** (including ADRs like ASML or MELI). Ratios and indices fall back to a simple line chart.
</Note>

## The chart

A clean candlestick chart by default, with the price scale on the **right** (TradingView-style). The toolbar groups everything into a few tidy dropdowns so it never gets cluttered, each showing a count of what's active.

### Timeframes

`1D · 1W · 1M · 3M · 6M · YTD · 1Y · 3Y · 5Y · 10Y · Custom`. We load **ten years** of history behind whatever window you pick, so you can always pan back past the chosen range to see more.

### Bar type

The **Candle ▾** menu switches how price is drawn:

- **Candle** — classic candlesticks, with a volume sub-panel
- **HA** — Heikin-Ashi, the smoothed candles that make trends easier to read
- **Line** — a simple close line
- **Area** — the close line with a filled gradient

### Studies

The **Studies ▾** menu holds every technical overlay in one place:

- **Moving averages** — quick-add **SMA** or **EMA** at 20 / 50 / 200, or type any custom period (say `EMA 34`). Each wears its own colour and a removable chip, and they stick across the names you open.
- **Bollinger Bands** — 20-period, 2σ band edges
- **VWAP** — anchored volume-weighted average price from the window start
- **RSI** — 14-period, in its own sub-panel with 30/70 guides
- **MACD** — 12/26/9 histogram + MACD/signal lines, in its own sub-panel

### Markers

The **Markers ▾** menu drops event flags right on the price:

- **Calls** — your tracked writers' entries as **▲ long / ▼ short**, anchored to the bar they called; hover any marker for the writer, date, and return (same-day calls stack into one popup)
- **Earnings** — quarterly report flags carrying the reported EPS
- **Dividends** — ex-dividend dates and per-share amounts

### Scale & compare

- **Log / %** — flip the price axis to logarithmic, or to **% change** from the window start (so a multi-ticker comparison all lines up at 0%)
- **Compare** — type any US-listed ticker to overlay it; add several, each a coloured chip you can remove, and switch between **separate** price axes or a **shared** one

### Navigating the chart

It moves the way you'd expect a real chart to:

- **Scroll** to zoom toward your cursor
- **Drag the time axis** left/right to expand or contract the window (it follows your pull)
- **Drag the chart** to pan freely; **double-click the price axis** to refit, **double-click the chart** to reset to the full range
- **Hover** for a crosshair with the full **OHLC, volume, and Δ%** readout
- **⊿ Measure** — drag between two points for the Δ price, Δ%, and number of bars / days

### Snapshot, share & export

Up in the header:

- **Share** — download the chart as a branded **PNG**, copy that image straight to your clipboard, grab a **share link** that reopens the exact view (ticker, timeframe, studies, scale, compares), or **export the visible OHLC to CSV**
- **Fullscreen** — expand the pop-out to fill the screen
- **TradingView** — pull the name up in TradingView if you'd rather work there

## The company panel

The panel on the left (collapse it with the **‹** chevron to give the chart the full width):

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Key stats">
    Price, market cap, P/E and EPS (TTM), dividend yield, RSI(14), today's volume and 30-day average — plus a **52-week range bar** showing where the price sits between its yearly high and low.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Performance">
    A colour-coded grid of returns across `1D · 1W · 1M · 3M · 6M · YTD · 1Y · 3Y · 5Y · 10Y`.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="About">
    A short business description, plus employees, headquarters, listing date, and a link to the company's site.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### In the Research

This is where the pop-out plugs into the [Writers corpus](/research/overview). When your tracked writers have weighed in on the name, you get:

- **A one-line pulse** — open `▲ long / ▼ short` calls, how many writers, and how many recent mentions
- **The calls** — each with its direction, the writer (and their track-record badge), the alpha vs SPY, the evidencing quote, and how many times they've reaffirmed it
- **Recent mentions** — the latest pieces *about* this name across Substack, YouTube, and X

It's the [Calls](/research/calls) positioning view, right next to the price.

### Ask Aura

Pinned to the bottom of the panel is an **Ask Aura** box — ask anything about the name (or markets in general) without leaving the chart. It already knows which ticker you're looking at, so "what's the bull case?" or "why is it moving today?" just work. It's the same engine as the full [AskAura](/research/ask) — streaming answers with cited sources, a **deep** toggle for heavier research, and multi-turn follow-ups.
