title: "Quant score"
description: "The −1 to +1 number that summarizes how risk-on or risk-off the market is right now."

The **quant score** is one number, between −1 and +1, that summarizes how risk-on or risk-off the market is at a given moment. It updates every five minutes during the trading day.

It's the spine of the [Daily Recap](/macro/daily-recap), the [Market Pulse](/macro/market-pulse) sidebar, and the [day's arc bar](/concepts/days-arc-bar).

## How to read it

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **+0.5 to +1.0** | Strong risk-on — equities up, vol low, credit tight, breadth wide |
| **+0.2 to +0.5** | Mild risk-on |
| **−0.2 to +0.2** | Neutral / mixed |
| **−0.5 to −0.2** | Mild risk-off |
| **−1.0 to −0.5** | Strong risk-off — equities down, vol up, credit widening, defensives bid |

## What goes into it

Eight buckets, weighted and combined:

- **Equities** — index returns, sector dispersion
- **Volatility** — VIX, MOVE, term structure, [vol richness](/concepts/vol-richness)
- **Credit** — HY spreads (HYG vs SPY)
- **[Breadth](/concepts/breadth)** — A/D, % above moving averages
- **Yields** — front-end vs long-end direction
- **Gold** — defensive haven flow
- **Dollar** — DXY direction
- **Sector tilts** — defensive vs cyclical leadership

No AI in the calculation — it's a deterministic weighted average. AI is layered on top later for the written recap.

## Why −1 to +1

Bounded scale means today's score is directly comparable to last Tuesday's, last quarter's, last year's. The percentile next to the score (e.g., "57th %ile") tells you where today sits in the historical distribution.
