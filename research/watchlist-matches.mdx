The **watchlist matches** view filters the [Research](/research/overview) feed to **only items mentioning tickers in your active watchlist**. It's the highest signal-to-noise way to use Research — by definition, every item shown is relevant to something you care about.

## How to enable it

From the [Research browse page](/research/browsing):

- The active **Watchlist** picker (top control) selects which list to filter against
- Toggle "Watchlist only" — feed compresses to just hits

When no watchlist is active, the toggle has no effect — you'll see the full feed.

## Primary vs mentioned

Each match is tagged as either:

- **Primary** — the piece is *about* this ticker (or it's one of the main subjects). Heavier signal.
- **Mentioned** — the ticker came up in passing (e.g., "names like NVDA and AMD..."). Lighter signal.

Filter to Primary-only for the highest signal. Mentioned is useful for spotting when a name suddenly starts showing up everywhere.

## The daily recap chatter strip

The [Daily Recap](/macro/daily-recap) embeds a **chatter strip** in the watchlist context — a horizontal list of the most-discussed names from your watchlist over the last 72 hours, with mention counts and trend arrows.

Useful for:
- Spotting where attention concentrated overnight
- Catching names that are heating up before the price moves obviously

## How most people use it

- **Pre-market routine** — open Research, set the active watchlist to "main book," toggle Watchlist only. Read everything that touched a holding overnight.
- **Theme tracking** — keep a "themes" watchlist (e.g., AI infra names) separate from your main book; check the matches view to track what's being said about the theme broadly.
- **Daily Recap chatter strip** — quick scan after the close; what was your portfolio's research footprint today?
