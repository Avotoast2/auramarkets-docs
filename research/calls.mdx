**Calls** turns the Writers corpus into a scoreboard. Every time a tracked writer takes a directional stance on a ticker — long or short, on Substack, X, or YouTube — it becomes a **tracked call**: entered at the price when they said it, scored against SPY ever since.

Writers are tracked as **one identity across platforms** — a call tweeted from an X account and a deep-dive on the same person's Substack land under the same name, so a track record is the writer's whole record, not one channel's slice.

It's the answer to two questions you can't get from a raw feed:

- **Who's actually been right?** Not who posts the most — who's beaten the market on the calls they made.
- **Where is everyone positioned?** For any ticker, how many of your writers are long vs short right now, and which of them have a track record worth weighting.

Calls is the **default landing tab** under Research → Writers.

<Note>
This is a *model*, not anyone's brokerage statement. A call is entered at the close on/after the writer's mention and held — it's a consistent yardstick for comparing takes, not a claim about what anyone actually traded.
</Note>

## What counts as a call

Each piece of content runs through a stance pass that asks, per primary ticker: is the writer **long**, **short**, or just mentioning it? Only genuine directional views become calls. A passing reference doesn't.

Re-mentions are handled so frequent posters don't get a hundred duplicate "calls" on the same name:

| Event | What happens |
|---|---|
| **First mention** | Opens the call (long or short) at that day's close |
| **Same-direction re-mention** | Reaffirms the open call — bumps a counter, no new position |
| **Opposite-direction mention** | Closes the call (reversed) and opens the opposite one |
| **6 months pass** | The call expires — its headline return freezes at the 6-month mark |

## The writers leaderboard (left)

Every tracked writer, ranked. Four columns:

| Column | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **CALLS** | Total calls on record |
| **HIT** | Share of scored calls that beat the market (positive alpha) |
| **ALPHA** | Median market-beating edge per call — the headline track-record number |

**Click any header to sort**; click again to reverse. The ALPHA and HIT columns keep writers with fewer than 3 scored calls below those with a real sample, so a lucky one-call writer can't top the board.

### The weather chips

A small **☂** or **☀** next to a writer's name grades whether their edge survives factor stress. Every call's alpha is bucketed by the **factor climate on its entry day** (the momentum basket's trailing-month move: unwind / quiet / momentum-on tape):

- **☂ all-weather** — median alpha stays positive even on calls opened into unwind tapes. Real edge, not factor beta.
- **☀ fair-weather** — positive alpha only when momentum is working; goes negative in unwinds. The writer may just be a momentum passenger — weight their calls accordingly when the factor tape turns.

Hover the chip for the actual split (median alpha and n per regime). No chip means not enough calls in the stress bucket to say (fewer than 3 unwind-tape entries).

### The activity dots

A coloured dot on the left edge flags recent activity at a glance:

- **● solid green** — opened a *new* call in the last 3 days (fresh conviction)
- **○ hollow green** — only *re-mentioned* an existing open call in the last 3 days (still on it, no new position)
- **no dot** — quiet

The **Active / All** toggle (top of the column) floats writers with recent calls to the top by default; switch to **All** for a pure column sort with no activity priority.

### Search writers

The **search box** at the top of the column narrows the leaderboard to whoever you type — by name or publication — and stays pinned as you scroll. It uses the same stacked-tag behaviour as the call search ([more below](#stacking-search-tags)), so you can layer terms to home in fast.

### Favorites

Hit the **☆** next to a writer to favorite them. Favorites jump to the very top of the leaderboard — above the activity tiers — with a faint divider separating them from everyone else, so the writers you actually follow are always in view. Favorites are saved to your account and follow you across devices.

## Writer grades

The leaderboard tells you who's been right; **grades** tell you whose track record is strong enough to act on — in either direction.

Every writer's calls are measured for **forward excess return vs SPY at 21 sessions** after the call (5- and 63-session windows too). Writers with **at least 8 calls** and statistically meaningful results earn a tier:

| Tier | Meaning |
|---|---|
| <span style={{color:"#d4af37"}}>**FOLLOW**</span> (gold) | Measurably good — their calls beat the market by a margin the sample supports |
| <span style={{color:"#ef7a78"}}>**FADE**</span> (red) | Reliably wrong — a genuine contrary indicator. That's information too. |
| *ungraded* | Everyone else — NEUTRAL or not enough calls yet. No badge, no verdict. |

Badges sit right next to writer names on the leaderboard. **Hover one** for the evidence: average vs SPY, hit rate, and sample size.

Grades recompute **weekly** and sharpen as the ledger grows — a writer who's ungraded today may earn (or earn *against*) a badge as their calls season.

<Note>
Each call is also tagged **TA**, **FA**, or **MIXED** by the extraction model — whether the writer's case rests on technicals, fundamentals, or both. Useful when you want to weight a chartist's breakout call differently from a fundamental thesis.
</Note>

## The call list (right)

Every call, newest first — company logo, ticker, direction, badges, then the writer on their own line (with their [FOLLOW/FADE grade](#writer-grades) when they have one), the verbatim quote that evidenced it, and live P&L (entry → current, with the return and alpha).

- **Newest / Top Gainers** — sort by recency or by return
- **Filters** — everything else lives in one popover; see [Filtering & drill-downs](#filtering--drill-downs)
- **Search** — a box at the top of the column narrows the list by ticker, writer, publication, or thesis text ([stacking tags](#stacking-search-tags) below)

## Filtering & drill-downs

### The Filters popover

One button next to the sort tabs holds every list filter; a **count badge** on it shows how many are active, so a narrowed list is never mistaken for the full one. **Reset filters** clears the lot.

| Filter | Options | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Direction** | All / Longs / Shorts | Only one side of the book |
| **Status** | All / Open / Closed | Closed = expired + reversed |
| **Result** | All / Winners / Losers / **α+** | Winners/Losers by headline return; **α+** keeps only calls beating SPY over their own window, direction-adjusted — up isn't enough |
| **Opened** | All / 7d / 30d / 90d | Recency window on the open date |
| **Writer tier** | All / FOLLOW / FADE | Only calls from writers with that [grade](#writer-grades) — FADE is the "take the other side" screen |
| **Macro calls** | on/off | See [Macro toggle](#macro-toggle) |
| **Favorites first** | on/off (default on) | Floats your favorited writers' calls to the top; the sort still applies within each group |
| **Reaffirmed only (×2+)** | on/off | Only calls the writer has re-mentioned while open — conviction, not a drive-by |
| **Tracked only** | on/off | Hides untracked and pending rows |

Filters combine with each other and with search tags — *FOLLOW-tier + Shorts + Open* is three clicks and a genuinely interesting list.

### Three clicks per row

Each call row routes clicks by where you land:

- **The ticker** → the [ticker focus panel](#ticker-focus) — positioning tally + annotated chart across *all* writers
- **The writer's name** → that writer's complete call history (same as clicking their leaderboard row; click again to clear)
- **Anywhere else on the row** → the **call modal**: the thesis blown up with a stats strip (entry price and date, last or frozen price, return, α vs SPY, running return, status and re-mentions) above the ticker's full price chart with **▲/▼ entry triangles for every call on that name** — this call's own triangle highlighted — so one writer's entry is always seen in the context of everyone else's

### Stacking search tags

Both search boxes — calls (right) and writers (left) — work the same way. Type a term and hit **Enter** and it pins as a little **tag**; stack as many as you like, each with an **✕** to drop it (or **Backspace** on an empty box to pop the last one). Typing also filters live before you commit.

As you type, a **suggestion dropdown** offers matches — tickers (with company names and call counts) and writers — so typing `intel` finds INTC even when no call text contains the word "intel". Pick one with a click or ↑/↓ + Enter; plain Enter with nothing highlighted still commits your raw text as a free-text tag.

Tags are **AND** — a row has to match **every** tag — so they narrow as you add them. Search `Druckenmiller`, then `NVDA`, and you're left with exactly that writer's NVDA calls. The calls search matches ticker, writer, publication, or thesis text; the writers search matches name or publication.

<Note>
A **writer or ticker tag pulls that name's complete history** from the server — same as clicking their leaderboard row — so a writer with 89 calls shows all 89, not just the ones recent enough to be in the default list. Free-text tags (thesis words like "foundry") filter whatever's loaded.
</Note>

### Ticker focus

Clicking a ticker opens a focus panel with two things straight out of the "where's everyone positioned" playbook:

- **Positioning tally** — a `N LONG / M SHORT` bar across all open calls on that ticker
- **Annotated price chart** — the price line with a marker at every writer's entry (**▲** long, **▼** short), plus a permanent label on the single biggest-magnitude call so there's always one headline takeaway

**Hover any marker** for the full call — who made it, the date, the entry price, the return, and the thesis quote. When several writers called the same name on the same day, their markers stack on one point and a single hover lists them all.

It works the other way too: **hover a call row in the list below** and its marker lights up on the chart (with a guideline down to the date), so you can match a specific call to its spot on the price path without hunting.

When calls bunch up in a busy stretch, **zoom in to pull them apart**:

- **Drag the time axis** left/right to contract/expand the window (it follows your cursor), or **scroll** to zoom toward wherever you're pointing
- **Drag the chart** to pan across time
- **Double-click** to snap back to the full range

### Call badges

| Badge | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **pre-IPO** | Priced on Hyperliquid perps (see below) |
| **pending** | Mentioned too recently to price yet — e.g. opened over a weekend; it prices on the next market close |
| **untracked** | No price source — delisted, foreign-listed, or non-tradeable |
| **expired** | Hit the 6-month horizon; headline return is frozen |
| **reversed** | The writer flipped direction; the call closed at the flip |
| **first close** | The mention predated available price data; entry is the first close we could get |

## Pre-IPO names

When a writer calls a company before it's public — SpaceX, OpenAI, Anthropic — there's no stock to price against. Calls uses **Hyperliquid's pre-IPO perpetual markets** for these, so the call is scored from day one and carries straight through the eventual IPO. These show a **pre-IPO** badge.

## Macro toggle

By default the list focuses on single names — single-name stocks, pre-IPO names, and crypto. Flip **Macro calls** on in the [Filters popover](#the-filters-popover) to bring in futures, equity indices, rates, and FX when you want the whole picture.

## How the numbers work

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Alpha — the edge per call">
    For each call, alpha is the call's return **minus SPY's return over the same window**, sign-adjusted for direction (a short that falls while the market rises is positive alpha). Each call is scored over its own life — up to its 6-month freeze if closed, or entry-to-today if still open. The **ALPHA** column is the *median* across all of a writer's scored calls. There's no fixed rolling window — it spans their whole track record.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Hit rate">
    The share of a writer's scored calls with positive alpha — i.e. how often they beat the market, not how often the call simply went up.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Which calls get scored">
    Every priced call counts from day one — closed calls at their frozen value, open calls at their running value. A brand-new call contributes near-zero alpha until the market actually moves, so fresh positions barely move the averages on their own.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Benchmark">
    Everything is measured against **SPY**. Raw return in a rising market flatters everyone; alpha vs SPY is the part that's actually skill.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What it's not

- **Not recommendations** — it's a scoreboard of other people's stated views, nothing more
- **Not anyone's real P&L** — it's a consistent model (enter at mention, hold), not a trade blotter
- **Not realtime** — calls and prices update on the daily cron
